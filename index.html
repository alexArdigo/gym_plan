Goblet Squat
3 × 12 Quads · Glutes · Core
Use the Smith machine or hack squat machine. Both guide the movement safely.
How to do it
  1. Smith machine: set bar at shoulder height, step under it, feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly out.
  2. Unrack the bar, brace core, and sit down keeping chest tall and knees tracking over toes.
  3. Go until thighs are parallel to the floor or as deep as comfortable.
  4. Drive through heels back to standing. Squeeze glutes at the top. Re-rack.
  5. Hack squat machine: load light, sit into the pad with feet on the platform, same squat movement.
Key tips
  • Start with just the bar (Smith) or empty sled (hack squat). Learn the movement first.
  • Knees must track over toes throughout — don't let them collapse inward.
No machine available — use a dumbbell (goblet squat).
How to do it
  1. Hold one dumbbell vertically at chest height with both hands.
  2. Stand feet shoulder-width apart, toes angled slightly out.
  3. Brace core, sit down between your knees pushing knees out over toes.
  4. Go until thighs are parallel to the floor or as deep as comfortable.
  5. Drive through heels back to standing. Squeeze glutes at the top.
Key tips
  • If heels rise, place a small plate or folded towel under them.
  • Start light (4–6kg). Form first.

Romanian Deadlift / Leg Curl
3 × 10 Hamstrings · Glutes · Lower back
⚠️ Disc history: use the lying or seated leg curl machine — same muscles, zero spinal load. Safer option for you.
How to do it
  1. Lying leg curl: lie face down on the machine, position the pad just above your heels.
  2. Curl your heels toward your glutes as far as comfortable. Squeeze hamstrings at the top.
  3. Lower slowly back to start — take 3 seconds on the way down.
  4. Seated leg curl: sit in the machine, same principle — curl legs down against the resistance, squeeze, return slowly.
  5. Do 3 sets of 10 reps.
Key tips
  • Control the return — don't let the weight snap back.
  • Focus on feeling the hamstrings working, not just moving the weight.
No machine — use dumbbells for RDL. Read the disc safety note carefully.
How to do it
  1. Stand feet hip-width, dumbbells in each hand in front of thighs.
  2. With a slight knee bend, push your hips BACK (not down) as you lower dumbbells along your legs.
  3. Keep back flat and chest up — spine neutral throughout.
  4. Lower until you feel a hamstring stretch (roughly shin level). STOP if your back rounds.
  5. Drive hips forward to return. Squeeze glutes at top.
Key tips
  • The movement is "hips back" not "bend forward."
  • Start very light (4–8kg each). Stop immediately if you feel anything in your lower back or legs.

Glute Bridge / Hip Thrust
3 × 15 Glutes · Hamstrings
Use the hip thrust machine if your gym has one — it's ideal for this exercise.
How to do it
  1. Hip thrust machine: sit into the machine with your upper back against the pad.
  2. Feet flat on the platform, hip-width apart.
  3. Drive through your heels and squeeze your glutes to push the platform up.
  4. Your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees at the top.
  5. Hold 1 second at the top, squeezing hard. Lower slowly. Repeat 15 times.
Key tips
  • The squeeze at the top is what activates the glutes — don't just move the weight.
  • If no hip thrust machine, the floor version below is equally effective.
No machine — floor version is just as effective for this exercise.
How to do it
  1. Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor hip-width apart.
  2. Press your lower back gently into the floor.
  3. Drive through heels, squeeze glutes to lift hips until body forms a straight line shoulder to knee.
  4. Hold 1 second at the top. Lower slowly. Repeat 15 times.
Key tips
  • Don't arch your lower back at the top — keep core braced.
  • If you feel it in your lower back instead of your glutes, move feet slightly further from your hips.

Side-lying Clamshells
3 × 15 each side Hip abductors · Glute medius
Use the hip abduction machine (the one where you sit and push your knees apart).
How to do it
  1. Sit in the hip abduction machine with pads on the outside of your knees.
  2. Keep your back flat against the pad and core braced.
  3. Push your knees apart against the resistance as far as comfortable.
  4. Hold 1 second at the widest point. Return slowly — don't let the weight slam back.
  5. 15 reps, 3 sets.
Key tips
  • Use light weight — this is a stabiliser muscle, not a power muscle.
  • The slow return (3 seconds) is where a lot of the work happens.
No machine — floor clamshell.
How to do it
  1. Lie on your side, hips stacked, knees bent ~45 degrees, feet together.
  2. Keeping feet together and hips from rolling back, lift your top knee up like a clam opening.
  3. Hold 1 second at the top. Lower slowly. 15 reps then flip sides.
Key tips
  • You should feel this on the outside of the working hip, not in your lower back.
  • Keep core braced so hips don't roll backward as you lift.

Dead Bug
3 × 8 each side Deep core · Spine stability
No machine for this one — it's a floor exercise. Find a mat in the gym stretching area.
How to do it
  1. Lie on back on a mat, knees bent 90°, feet off floor (shins parallel to ground), arms pointing straight up.
  2. Press lower back firmly into the mat — keep it there throughout.
  3. Slowly lower your RIGHT arm behind your head while simultaneously extending your LEFT leg out straight.
  4. Stop before either touches the floor. Return both. Repeat opposite side.
  5. 8 reps each side, 3 sets.
Key tips
  • Lower back MUST stay pressed to floor — this is the whole point of the exercise.
  • Go slow: 3–4 seconds down. Don't rush.
  • If lower back lifts off the mat, reduce your range of movement.
Same exercise — no machine version is the only version.
How to do it
  1. Lie on back on a mat, knees bent 90°, feet off floor, arms pointing straight up.
  2. Press lower back firmly into mat.
  3. Slowly lower RIGHT arm + extend LEFT leg simultaneously.
  4. Return both before they touch the floor. Switch sides. 8 reps each side.
Key tips
  • Lower back stays pressed to the floor throughout.
  • Slow and controlled — this is a core stability exercise, not cardio.

Seated Row
3 × 12 Mid-back · Rear delts · Biceps
Use the seated cable row machine or a chest-supported row machine.
How to do it
  1. Cable row: sit at the machine, feet on footrests, slight knee bend, back straight.
  2. Grip the handle with both hands, arms extended.
  3. Pull the handle toward your lower chest/upper abdomen, driving elbows back.
  4. At the end, squeeze shoulder blades together as if holding a pencil between them. Hold 1 sec.
  5. Slowly extend arms back. Don't let your torso rock forward and back.
  6. Chest-supported row machine: lie chest-down on the pad, same pulling motion — even safer for your back.
Key tips
  • The scapular squeeze at the end is the most important part for posture.
  • Chest-supported row is the safest option for disc history — removes all spinal load.
No machine — use dumbbells for a single-arm row.
How to do it
  1. Place your right knee and right hand on a flat bench for support.
  2. Hold a dumbbell in your left hand, arm hanging straight down.
  3. Pull the dumbbell up toward your hip, driving your elbow back and up.
  4. Squeeze your back at the top. Lower slowly. 12 reps, then switch sides.
Key tips
  • Keep your back flat — don't rotate your torso to lift the weight.
  • Think "elbow to hip", not "lift the dumbbell."

Incline Chest Press
3 × 12 Upper chest · Shoulders · Triceps
Use the incline chest press machine — easier to control than dumbbells and safer for beginners.
How to do it
  1. Adjust the seat so the handles are at upper chest level.
  2. Set the backrest to an incline angle (usually 30–45 degrees).
  3. Grip the handles, elbows at roughly 45 degrees to your body.
  4. Press forward and slightly up until arms are nearly extended — don't lock elbows.
  5. Return slowly (3 seconds) back to start. Repeat 12 times.
Key tips
  • Keep your back flat against the pad throughout — don't arch.
  • Start at a very low weight to learn the movement first.
No machine — use dumbbells on an incline bench.
How to do it
  1. Set bench to 30–45 degree incline. Sit with back against pad, dumbbells on thighs.
  2. Kick dumbbells to shoulder level as you lie back, palms facing forward.
  3. Press dumbbells up and slightly together above your upper chest.
  4. Lower slowly back to chest level, elbows at ~45 degrees to your body. Repeat.
Key tips
  • Don't arch lower back off the bench — lower the weight if this happens.
  • Start very light (4–6kg each). Take 2–3 seconds on the way down.

Face Pulls ⭐
3 × 15 Rear delts · Upper back · Rotator cuff
Cable machine with rope attachment. This exercise requires the cable — no good machine substitute.
How to do it
  1. Set the cable pulley to face height or slightly above. Attach the rope.
  2. Grip each end of the rope, step back so there's tension in the cable.
  3. Pull the rope toward your face, driving elbows OUTWARD and back.
  4. At the end: hands at ear level, elbows flared out wide. Hold 1 sec.
  5. Slowly return. Keep torso upright throughout. 15 reps.
Key tips
  • Use very light weight — precision exercise, not a strength exercise.
  • Do NOT lean backward to pull. Torso stays vertical.
  • Elbows flared OUT — this is what activates the right muscles.
No cable machine — use resistance bands or rear delt fly with light dumbbells.
How to do it
  1. Rear delt fly (dumbbells): sit on the edge of a bench, lean forward so your chest is near your knees.
  2. Hold a light dumbbell in each hand hanging below you, slight bend in elbows.
  3. Raise both arms out to the sides until they are parallel to the floor.
  4. Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top. Lower slowly. 15 reps.
Key tips
  • Use very light dumbbells (2–4kg). This is not a heavy exercise.
  • The motion is "open like wings" — not lifting straight up.

Lat Pulldown
3 × 12 Lats · Biceps · Rear delts
Lat pulldown machine — already a machine exercise.
How to do it
  1. Sit at the lat pulldown machine, adjust the thigh pad so it holds you down firmly.
  2. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width, overhand grip (palms away from you).
  3. Lean back slightly (~15–20 degrees), then pull the bar down toward your upper chest.
  4. Drive your elbows down and back. Squeeze your back muscles. Hold 1 sec.
  5. Slowly let the bar back up until arms are nearly straight. Repeat 12 times.
Key tips
  • Always pull to the FRONT — never behind your neck.
  • Think "elbows to pockets" — that creates the back movement.
  • No swinging or momentum — control every rep.
No lat pulldown machine — use assisted pull-up machine or resistance bands.
How to do it
  1. Assisted pull-up machine: set the counterweight (higher weight = easier), grip the handles.
  2. Pull yourself up until your chin is above the bar. Lower slowly.
  3. If no assisted machine, attach a resistance band to a high anchor point and do band pull-downs.
Key tips
  • The assisted pull-up machine is found in most gyms — ask staff if you can't find it.

Step-ups / Leg Press
3 × 10 each leg Quads · Glutes · Hip stability
Use the leg press machine — safer for beginners and eliminates balance challenges.
How to do it
  1. Sit in the leg press machine, place feet on the platform hip-width apart, roughly in the middle.
  2. Press the platform away from you until legs are nearly extended — don't lock knees.
  3. Slowly return (3 seconds) to the start position with knees at ~90 degrees.
  4. For single-leg variation: use one leg at a time, same motion. Much lighter weight.
  5. 10 reps, 3 sets.
Key tips
  • Start very light — the leg press can feel easy but your joints need time to adapt.
  • Don't let knees cave inward as you press.
No machine — step-ups on a bench or box.
How to do it
  1. Stand in front of a gym bench or box (about knee height — lower if needed).
  2. Place your right foot fully on the bench.
  3. Drive through your right heel to step up, bringing left foot up to stand on bench.
  4. Step back down with control, leading with the left foot.
  5. Complete 10 reps on right leg, then switch.
Key tips
  • Push through the heel of the top foot, not the toes.
  • Start bodyweight only. Add dumbbells only when fully stable.

  • Normal to wobble at first — practise near a wall for support.

Cytisine — What the Evidence Shows
▾
